Project Aanch

Concept

The Problem

  • Over 3 billion[1] people globally (~400 million[2] people in India) use solid fuels to cook or heat their homes on open fires or traditional stoves. Time spent in collecting these solid fuels is roughly 2 hours per woman per day. The highly efficient burning produces a range of health damaging pollutants such as carbon monoxide and fine particles.
  • Within poorly ventilated dwellings, the concentration of these pollutants can reach a 100 times the acceptable level. The WHO estimates that over 4.3 million[1] people (~0.9 million[2] in India) die annually as a result of this Indoor Air Pollution (IAP). These pollutants lead to respiratory, pulmonary, and vision problems.
  • The average Indian spends 9%[2] of their monthly income on fuel, which is growing at 9% per annum[2], above the current rate of inflation.
  • The younger females of households spend long hours on firewood collection and cooking, leaving lesser time for their education.

Our Approach

  • To deal with this scenario, we launched Project Aanch in February 2013. A through demographic study and survey led us to adopt the low smoke cookstove by Phillips and further modified it to solve the local issues.
  • We identified 5 women entrepreneurs in Bhati Mines, a rural community in Chatarpur to market, manufacture, and sell the stove.
  • These stoves with chimney and soot collector cut down IAP by 48%. As a result of the stoveâ€™s higher efficiency, cooking time is reduced by 50%[3] and 42 hours[3] (=62-21) are saved per customer per month.
  • Profits from sales of the stove makes our women entrepreneurs financially independent.
  • The time saved from advanced smokeless stove can now be utilized for education or other profit generating activities.
