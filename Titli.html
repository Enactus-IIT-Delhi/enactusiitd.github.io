Titli

Titli is a social entrepreneurship model which entails women as the primary stakeholder. Women are undoubtedly an integral part of any economy, and the menstrual cycle is a crucial biological aspect of their lives.However in India, discussions about menstruation is a social taboo and “Titli” works precisely on tackling this problem.Titli solves this problem by distributing machine manufactured sterilised sanitary pads across different parts of the country to women,especially in the rural areas.

Titli came into existence because of the belief shared by a group of 10 motivated students, that all it takes to lead the world towards sustainable growth is planned entrepreneurial effort and a never let go attitude. 88 percent of the Indian woman population, even today do not use sanitary napkins. They resort to unhygienic measures like torn clothes, rags and ashes. These commodities cause severe health damage to women in the form of reproductive tract infections. Rural women are in particular still apprehensive to the idea of an open discussion about menstruation. Most of these women are unaware of the self-harm they are causing. The major reasons for such an unaddressed turmoil are un-affordability and inaccessibility of sterilised sanitary napkins in such communities. Through Titli we solve both these problems comprehensively.

We have a product which is medically tested, biodegradable and with a longer usage life. The Titli Pads have been originally designed by Mahesh Khandelwal, a leading social entrepreneur of Uttar Pradesh. Focused research and development by our team has led to the better product. This product is sold at prices up to three times lower than the other pad selling companies. The challenge to accessibility is solved by the use of local NGOs as our sales and marketing partners. These NGOs which are already trusted and are involved thoroughly into the community help our product overcome obstacles of taboo and distrust at a very rapid rate. Our product is manufactured and sold by members of the community itself who are our entrepreneurs, giving it higher stakes in the eye of a rural woman. The problems of logistics are well addressed too by the strong collaboration we have with NGOs. The whole model is sustainable as the women are manufacturing napkins and selling it within their community and they are being supervised by the NGO. Hence even if our team phase out, it would not affect the functioning of the hub.

The price of our pads are 3 times lower then the market price making it easily affordable by people living in rural areas. Our model is self sustainable. And the presence of the sales women in our model make it easily accessible for women.

Social Impact : Our model is generating a regular employment for women. These women earlier used to be housewives but now they can contribute significantly in their family income. Since the whole production unit would be run by them therefore with time they would learn the skills of marketing, manufacturing and selling. This would eventually make them confident, independent and true entrepreneurs. Apart from the entrepreneurs, the other women of the community are being made available a very affordable sanitary napkin. Hence they can easily shift to it without being concerned about the cost.
Environmental Impact : Since our napkins are biodegradable hence it is definitely a better alternative to the market product in terms of being environment friendly.
Economical Impact :The revenue is generated from the sales of the sanitary napkins which is a reliable source of income for women entrepreneurs.
