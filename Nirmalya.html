Nirmalya

Nirmalya is an initiative by IIT Delhi students which aims to transform the current waste management industry and make it socially, economically and environmentally favourable.

The problem our team is trying to address is Waste Management. Delhi alone is producing 9000 metric tons of waste everyday and 95% of this is dumped into landfills and water bodies. Landfills in Delhi are carrying 4 times more waste than their capacity. The result is pollution levels 158 times more than the admissible limit. In reality, just 20% of this waste produced in India actually needs to go to landfills; rest can be recycled or processed into compost or Biogas. There are more than 20 Million rag pickers in India. They come from different parts of the country, because the rural economy has not been able to sustain them. If they have no other support system, then they invariably end up doing this work. But in India, the people who handle our waste are invisible: we do not know them and do not acknowledge their role in our society. They face many problems from police, from us in form of meaningless stereotypes and from middlemen in form of exploitation.

We have come up with a solution where waste is managed locally in a decentralised way, the waste segregation is ensured, waste is properly reused and the revenue thus generated is used to provide garbage collectors a better financial status and working conditions. First we aware the residents of the society to segregate there waste in two different section wet and dry waste. Then we set up a waste collection service and ensure waste is being collected in segregated form. After that we set up local waste processing plants in each societies in collaboration with Residents Welfare Association( RWA) and SDMC to prepare compost of biodegradable waste and recycle the dry waste. And all the day waste is then sold off to recycling industries. For our operations, we provide employment to unemployed people from economically backward ragpicker community.

Traditional composting is not successful at a community level because of the smell associated with it which disturbs people living around that area. To reduce the odour we are using an enzyme solution that breakdowns curtain gases which are responsible for odour. This ensures smell less composting in that area thus reducing the residents biggest concern.

Social Impact : Our model is creating a regular employment for the ragpickers. These people who earlier had very little and uneven income would now have a sense of job security. On an average, we are employing 5 people for every hub in this one works in composting plant and other 4 collects waste from households. These ragpickers now also have a higher income then they had previously.
Environmental Impact : From just one society we would save more than 352 ton of waste per year which will significantly reduce land and air pollution. We are reducing the carbon dioxide emission by 234 metric tonnes a year which would help us bringing down the air pollution levels significantly. Also, the pollution caused by the trucks to take this garbage to the landfills is also being avoided.
Economical Impact : Other than the revenue generated by composting, we are also saving the money which goes into waste management. Transportation to landfills alone costs Rs 1000 per ton, which means over 3.5 lakh saved per year in one society. This acts as an big incentive for South Delhi Municipal Corporation to help us.
