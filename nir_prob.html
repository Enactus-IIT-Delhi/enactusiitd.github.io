Nirmalya

Nirmalya is an initiative by IIT Delhi students which aims to transform the current waste management industry and make it socially, economically and environmentally favourable.

  • All
  • Problem
  • Composting
  • Hubs
  • Impact

The problem our team is trying to address is Waste Management. Delhi alone is producing 9000 metric tons of waste everyday and 95% of this is dumped into landfills and water bodies. Landfills in Delhi are carrying 4 times more waste than their capacity. The result is pollution levels 158 times more than the admissible limit. In reality, just 20% of this waste produced in India actually needs to go to landfills; rest can be recycled or processed into compost or Biogas. There are more than 20 Million rag pickers in India. They come from different parts of the country, because the rural economy has not been able to sustain them. If they have no other support system, then they invariably end up doing this work. But in India, the people who handle our waste are invisible: we do not know them and do not acknowledge their role in our society. They face many problems from police, from us in form of meaningless stereotypes and from middlemen in form of exploitation.
